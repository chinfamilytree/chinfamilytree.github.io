Course Syllabus

Read the course syllabus information below carefully and email me [ gene_chin@fitnyc.edu ] the last sentence near the end of this page, filled in with appropriate content. Use "Contract" as the subject.

Descriptive Information

  1. CG214 Web Page Construction
    Students learn many aspects of constructing and implementing web pages, from content creation through production. Current HTML and web design software, such as Dreamweaver, is used to create deliverable web pages.
  2. Rationale of Catalog Description
    The rationale of the catalog description is to give students a general view of the course content.
  3. Intended Audience
    This course is for students who wish to create their own online website.
  4. Statement of Prerequisites
    CG111 or approval of chairperson
  5. Credits earned / hours / meeting place
    1.5 credit / 1 hr 50 min / in lab as assigned in catalog
  6. Safety & Health concerns
    No eating or drinking is permitted in computer laboratories. Students with learning, psychological, hearing, visual, physical/mobility, and medical disabilities are invited to meet with the staff of FIT-ABLE, the College’s Office of Disability Services in A570 or call 212.217.4090.
  7. Classroom etiquette-faculty expectations
    All cellular phones are to be turned off in classrooms and computer laboratories. Printing is allowed only if requested by the student and granted by the instructor. If a student has a question, the student must raise his or her hand and be recognized by the instructor before speaking.
  8. Computer/internet etiquette
    Minimize sending e-mails. Users usually get more than they can handle.
    Avoid flame war, i.e., prolonged exchange of hostile messages
    Avoid electronic spam, i.e., unsolicited bulk messages indiscriminately.
    Avoid typing in all capital letters, which is the equivalent of shouting.
    Be brief.
    Be polite.
    Use descriptive subject lines.
    Stay on topic.

Course Goals and Learning Outcomes

By the end of the semester, the student should have an understanding of how to construct a website and apply global formatting as well as localized formatting to web pages.

Instructional Procedures

Each class will begin by a roll call followed by a brief review of the previous lesson in which students are encouraged to ask questions. Following the review will be a lecture and a demonstration of new skills. Students are encouraged to follow the demonstration with their computers to obtain practical skills. Students are also encouraged to take advantage of free tutoring provided by the Academic Skills Center in Room A608B or call 212.217.4080.

Course Requirements

There are eight (8) required homework assignments and one extensive final project.

Evaluation of students - Methods of assessment

Each homework assignment is due at the beginning of the class when the class next meets. The instructor will check each student's homework. Late submissions are not credited. Each homework assignment is worth 7% of the final grade. Thus all the homework assignments will be a total of 56% of the final grade. The other 44% is a comprehensive final project. The student may use Dreamweaver and/or HTML/CSS (using a simple text processor, such as Notepad or TextEdit) to construct his/her final project. The entire site must be under 8 MB. The project must not contain any of the instructor's demo lists, sounds, or images.

Grading

Homework is worth 56% of the final grade and the comprehensive final project is worth 44% of the final grade. There is no extra credit for extra work. There are no exceptions to these rules.

Attendance Policy

Attendance will be taken at the beginning of each class. Students who come in later than 25 minutes after the class started will be marked absent.
3 unexcused latenesses = one absence
3 unexcused absences = one half grade decrease (e.g. an A becomes an A-)
More than 3 unexcused absences a student will receive a failing grade.

Late work and missed exams or presentations

Homework assignments must be handed in on time to be credited. If the student does not submit the final project according to specifications at the beginning of the last session of class, the student, in good standing, will be given the opportunity to submit it no later than the fifth week of the following semester. A grade of “IN” (with student's concordance) will be submitted for this semester if the student is in good standing, that is, that all homework assignments have been completed, no excessive unexcused tardiness or unexcused absences. Otherwise, a student will be given a failing grade.

Critiques

When the instructor is checking homework, the student is encouraged to ask questions, if any, concerning the homework.
