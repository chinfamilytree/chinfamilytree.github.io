Hierarchy of Styles

Let's use the h2 tag in this document to illustrate the hierarchy of styles from lowest to highest: browser default, external style sheet, internal style sheet, and (the highest) inline styles. Both Ophelia's used h2.

Ophelia (could not be simulated)

First, the word Ophelia was times new roman, the size of a heading 2 (may be slightly different for different browsers) with black color.

Ophelia (possibly a slight change here)

Second, the css1.css external style sheet sets the size to exactly 24px, retaining the black color and time new roman.

Ophelia (no change here, inherited or cascading effect)

The internal style sheet could next change it, but since it was not specified, heading 2 would inherit the change that was made in the external style sheet. It has the cascading effect, from the first word of css (cascading style sheet).

Ophelia (definitely changed here)

The final effect was made in the inline style which changed the font to Comic Sans MS (if this font is not found on a user's computer, then a cursive font would be used). The size was changed to 29 pixels and the font color was changed to #88B2A2 (quite a bit of green, a fear amount of blue, and less red).
